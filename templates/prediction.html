Diabetes Risk Factors and Statistics

Age Risk Factors

Your risk for diabetes increases the older you are. After the age of 45, your risk for diabetes increases dramatically. As you get older, your body's insulin resistance increases, which will cause the body's blood sugar to rise. People tend to exercise less as they age, which can lead to weight gain, one of the factors that have the most impact on diabetes risk.

BMI Risk Factors

Excess weight is considered the most impactful risk factor for diabetes. Extra visceral fat, fat located around the abdomen, is linked to increased insulin resistance. Visceral fat secretes retinol-binding protein 4, which has been used to predict diabetes and cardiovascular disease in patients. Excess weight usually leads to exercising less often due to increased levels of fatigue and pressure on joints.

High Blood Pressure Risk Factors

High blood pressure is a indicator for diabetes as they share common causes. Both high blood pressure and diabetes can be caused by a sedentary lifestyle, obesity, stress, and insulin resistance. The reduced amount of insulin due to diabetes can result in glucose (blood sugar) accumulating in the bloodstream. Higher levels of blood sugar can damage blood vessels and cause them to harden, which increases blood pressure.

Physical Activity Risk Factors

Exercising at least 150 minutes a week can have profound effects on a person's health. Physical activity can help control weight, blood pressure, and blood sugar. Physical activity has shown to increase insulin sensitivity which can help counteract the increase in insulin resistance caused by diabetes.
